My Love for Food History

I gained an interest in food history when I was in high school. I've always loved to bake, so when my US history teacher gave us a project to look at any topic we wanted through the lens of US history, I chose food. I learned that the food people ate can tell us so much about the goings-on of the time. Even better than that, food helps me feel a real connection to the past and to my ancestors. It's not everyone's cup of tea, but I find food history fascinating!

Favorite Food History Resources

Here are some of my favorite resources for learning about food history.

  1. Websites
    • Food Timeline: My go-to starting point (and a great example of a website built with html!)
    • What's Cooking America: Food History and Legends: A great collection of stories and recipes.
    • Old School Pastry: A blog specializing in old desserts.
    • The Gallery of Regrettable Food: A little difficult to navigate, but this fun website covers the worst of food history.
  2. Old Cookbooks One of the best places to learn about food history is from old cookbooks and menus. These sites are places to find such items.
    • Open Library: Not limited to cookbooks! This is a great resource for many books, even textbooks.
    • Conrad N. Hilton Library, Culinary Insitute of America: A great resource for all things food history, including cookbooks, menus, farmer's bullitens, and more.
    • Peacock-Harper Culinary History Collection (Digitized): Virginia Tech has scanned many of their old cookbooks and uploaded them as pdfs available for everyone.
  3. YouTube Channels
    • Tasting History with Max Miller: A great food history channel that focuses on all time periods and cultures.
    • Townsends: An amazing channel focusing on all things colonial America, with a particular emphasis on 18th century cooking. (As a plus, all the videos are very relaxing.)
    • 200-Year-Old Recipes (How to Cook That): Ann Reardon of the channel How to Cook That does all sorts of videos, but she has several focusing on old-time recipes.
  4. Other Resources
    • Gastropod Podcast: Gastropod looks at food through the lens of science and history. This is one of my favorite resources for learning about food history.
